1. Executive Summary
Shailraj Travels is a pilgrimage tour operator based in Pune, Maharashtra. This document defines the complete Product Requirements and Full SEO Strategy to drive organic Google traffic, rank for high-intent pilgrimage keywords, and convert visitors into tour inquiries.
India's spiritual tourism sector is growing rapidly — faith-based travel now accounts for a significant share of domestic tourism and is expected to continue growing through 2026 and beyond. Competitors like Swastik Holiday, Thomas Cook India, and regional operators already have SEO-optimized pages ranking for the same keywords Shailraj Travels needs to capture.
This plan covers: keyword strategy, page architecture, on-page SEO specifications, content playbook, technical SEO, local SEO, link building, blog SEO, schema markup, and monthly execution timeline.

12. Review System
12.1 On-Site Review Flow
User submits review via on-site form (Full Name, Rating, Tour Taken, Review Text)
Review saved to website database with pending status
Admin receives email notification for moderation
Admin approves review — it appears on the tour page and is marked up with Review schema
12.2 Google Review Funnel
After tour completion, send WhatsApp message with Google Review link
On-site: Show 'Share Your Experience' button after review submission
Button copies review text to clipboard automatically
Then opens Google Business Profile review page in new tab
Display message: 'Your review has been copied. Please paste it on Google Reviews to help other devotees.'
IMPORTANT: Do NOT auto-submit to Google — this violates Google's policies
12.3 Review Schema
Implement AggregateRating schema on each tour page. Required fields: ratingValue, reviewCount, bestRating (5), worstRating (1). This displays star ratings directly in Google search results.
13. Mobile SEO Requirements
Over 75% of pilgrimage tour searches in India happen on mobile devices. Mobile-first indexing means Google primarily uses the mobile version of the site for ranking.
13.1 Mobile UX Requirements
Fully responsive design — tested on Android Chrome, iOS Safari, Samsung Browser
Touch targets (buttons, links) minimum 44x44px
Font size minimum 16px on body text — no zooming required
Sticky WhatsApp button: bottom-right, 60px circle, always visible on scroll
Sticky Call button: bottom-left, 60px circle, always visible on scroll
Hamburger menu with smooth slide-out navigation on mobile
Forms with large input fields, auto-correct off for name/phone fields
Image sliders with swipe gesture support
13.2 Mobile Performance
Enable AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) for blog articles if feasible
Implement service worker for offline caching of key pages
Preload hero images using <link rel='preload'>
Defer non-critical JavaScript
Use next-gen image formats (WebP with JPEG fallback)

3. City SEO Expansion
3.1 Strategy
Local intent keywords — searches like 'Ashtavinayak Yatra from Mumbai' — convert at significantly higher rates than generic keywords because the user has already decided they want to travel; they are just choosing a provider. City pages capture this bottom-of-funnel traffic at scale.
Each city page is a unique, fully-optimised landing page — NOT a template with swapped city names. Google penalises thin, duplicated city pages. Each must have:
Unique intro paragraph referencing the departure city and journey details
City-specific departure logistics (bus pickup points, train connections, flight options)
City-specific pricing (if applicable — transport costs differ by city)
Local testimonials from customers from that city (if available)
City-specific FAQ section (e.g. 'How long is the journey from Mumbai to Shirdi?')

Google AI Overviews (previously called SGE) appear at the top of search results for many informational queries. They pull content from multiple trusted sources and summarise the answer. A page that gets cited in an AI Overview can receive significant visibility even without a top organic ranking.

10.2 Review Response Templates
Every review — positive and negative — must receive a personalised response. Template principles:
Positive reviews: Thank by name, mention the specific tour, invite them back, include a keyword naturally
Negative reviews: Acknowledge, apologise, take conversation offline, show resolution commitment
